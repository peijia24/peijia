Our results informed us several things:

Many people have not had any experience with personal training

  • people thought it was too expensive and that personal fitness was not a high priority
  • Some were too shy and feared being judged by personal trainers
  • People were not interested in personal training or didnâ€™t have time

Personal trainers were seen as an informational and skill base to further improve fitness and training methods

  • Tended to use personal training services as a way to try or answer questions that is not clear through client's own research
  • People were interested in interacting with a personal trainer if they were openly available in their gym

Our idea.

When we asked users to reflect on what would interest them in actively seeking out a personal trainer or training service, many of them pointed out that:

  • services must be affordable
  • the personal trainer must be relatable, seem approachable to contact
  • potential clients would be much more inclined have a trial service with the trainer to decide if their personalities and training styles match well together
  • offer group services + prices so that people new to personal training feel more comfortable and get to know the instructor
