Eriko Semitsu Portfolio

Site Design & Development

freelance client project
(3 weeks, summer 2018)

Eriko Semitsu is an award-winning floral designer based in Vancouver. I met her when I first joined Celsia Floral in 2016, and followed closely in her footsteps as a mentee in learning floral design. Her art styles are influenced by traditional Japanese elements, as well as organic + botanical feelings.

For some time now, Eriko has been wanting to have a website to display her work and thoughts so that other designers and artists could see her perspective in floral design. However, she didn't want to just create one on Wordpress or Squarespace. This provided the perfect opportunity for me to create a personal website for her, which I took on as a challenge!

Introduction

Discovery

The exploration.

Eriko had a specific mood and feel she wanted to capture in her website, such as "raw, old, Wabi-Sabi, Eikoseisui(栄枯盛衰)". She wanted to convey an aesthetics like an old film picture that is very simple and as is.

*Eikoseisui means the ups and downs of life :)

Because the primary goal of this website was more of a personal collection, I decided not to go forward with research or following business goals.

Thus, we had several coffee chats about what kind of content she wanted to include on her site, which included: home page, gallery, thoughts page and an about page.

Creation

Design stuff.

There was no logo design needed as Eriko was not aiming to create a brand.

I asked Eriko to think of several ideas for a potential favicon logo, and she decided on a grey version of the Japanese flag.

Desktop wireframes.

Our primary focus for this site was focused on desktop development, although it would have been smarter to begin from mobile first design.

Following on how the IA was laid out and after several reiterations, we decided on creating these wireframes.

Check out the complete wireframes here!

Mockups.

Based off the new wireframes and IA, we integrated it with the brand guidelines to create a mockup of the finished product.

While Jennifer was happy with our mockups, I personally thought that the mockups could be a little more uniquely designed to create a fun and smooth user experience.

Here is another version of how the site could have looked, if we only had the time and skills then to develop it!

Development

Implementing WordPress.

I decided to hard code the website because I thought it would be easier to maintain and change than having to introduce Wordpress and its content management system to Eriko. She agreed with me, and I told her that the only difficulty was updating the images and thoughts page - although I could help her with it when needed.

As the sole project lead, I had full control over what I wanted to create. Beginning with a mobile first approach, I followed the approved wireframe designs and made it responsive over a collective time of 3 weeks.

Deliverables

Finally, the live website was finished! You can view the site here :)

Reflections

I think the biggest issue was deciding on the design and function of the website, which actually matured from a youthful and bright initial design to the current dark and moody one.
