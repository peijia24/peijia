The flowers.

Becoming a floral designer was sort of the first concrete step of re-exploring the design world, and remembering how much I enjoyed creating things in different challenges.

Reasons.

I'm inspired by nature in the changing seasons, and how cyles of growth come in different forms. Nothing remains the same, yet things feel familiar.

Thoughts.

Of course flowers are special in the sense of beauty, form, colours, smells and textures.

Working with them makes me happy because flowers are just what they are, and anyone can create anything with them. There's no such thing as the best flowers, or the best tools, or the perfect method of floral design.

Creating an experience for people through flowers, or any medium is what inspires me to keep learning and designing. You can see genuine emotions when people pick up the flowers, smell them, admire them and simply enjoy them.

And that's why I love what I do.

Process.

For me, I tend to design flowers first with understanding the mood, vision, or context of what is needed. Also one must consider the budget, supplies and timeline of what you are designing.

With that in mind, I like to create the structure of the design first, forming the foundation of the piece. That can be the structural frame of an installation, or the greenery base of a bouquet. From there, I add the larger focal florals while balancing the design with smaller, filler flowers. Check and adjust the flowers often, the design will form from planning and practice.

Reflections.

Before working in a flower shop, I always thought I had to do some form of formal education for anything, even for working with flowers. But that's not true at all, as I have met so many designers that came from a totally unrelated background.

Thank you.
