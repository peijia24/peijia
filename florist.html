The flowers.

Becoming a floral designer was sort of the first concrete step of re-exploring the design world, and remembering how much I enjoyed creating things in different challenges.

And think about it, if no one gets you flowers - make your own :-)

Reasons.

It's really different and fun being in the foral industry, because it's a niche area that caters mainly to wedding and event work. So often times, I work along with photographers and event planners to finalize big celebrations! And I get to meet floral designers from all over the world.

Thoughts.

Flowers might not be for everyone, but they are special in the sense of beauty, colours, smells and textures.

Working with flowers allows me to be creative with more just my eyes (sorry digital design), and it gives me another outlet that also results in making other people happy!

Creating an experience for people through flowers is enduring and special to that moment. So, yeah - it's pretty cool.

And that's why I love what I do.

Process.

For me, I tend to design flowers first with understanding the mood, vision, or context of what is needed. Also you must consider the budget, supplies and timeline of what you are designing.

So my steps:
- create the structure of the design (usually with the greenery and branches)
- then add in the larger focal flowers (ex. roses)
- finish it with small filler flowers. Ta da! ~

Reflections.

Honestly before working in a flower shop, I always thought you had to be formally trained for any design work...

Which is totally NOT true so go for whatever you want to do! Experience is the best practice ✨

Thank you.
