The flowers.

On the side, I'm also a floral designer at Celsia! ☺

To be honest, floral design was sort of the first "design-y" thing that set off my design journey, because I was creating a tangible product for a time, space and real people.

(It also gives me an outlet that doesn't involve sitting all day and utilizes 5/5 of my underused senses)

The industry.

It's a really fun and niche environment, and you get to work with a lot of cool creatives and unexpected people (like Artizia, Lululemon, secret?! celebrities *cough* lady gaga *cough* riverdale cast hehe)

My thoughts.

So, I'm not quite the story teller yet about why I love working with flowers. But they look pretty, they smell good and they're a piece of nature in today's modern world of plastic and pollution.

The process.

For me, I like to design flowers by first with understanding the mood/vision, or context of what is needed! Also you must consider the budget, supplies and timeline of what you are designing.

So my steps are:

  1. 1. prep the flowers and make sure they are what the client wants! Talk about their vision for the event and the purpose of the flowers
  2. 2. start big with the greenery, branches to focus on the structure
  3. 3. add in the flowers - try to group similar flowers together as how they would grow in nature

Ta da! ~
